## Notes on Sources

### Reliability Assessment

**High Reliability**:
- Peer-reviewed academic papers
- Documented ethnographic research
- Government wildlife reports
- Museum collections

**Medium Reliability**:
- Cryptozoological books by trained researchers
- Documented expedition reports
- Historical newspaper accounts
- Published oral traditions

**Use With Caution**:
- Undocumented online claims
- Sensationalist media reports
- Unverified witness accounts
- Conspiracy-oriented sources

### Indigenous Knowledge

Indigenous oral traditions represent sophisticated knowledge systems developed over centuries. While they may not always align with Western scientific epistemology, they deserve respectful consideration as legitimate ways of knowing and understanding the natural world.

Researchers should:
- Recognize oral tradition as valid knowledge
- Understand cultural context
- Avoid extractive research practices
- Compensate knowledge holders appropriately
- Share benefits of research with source communities
